# AI Matching Engine

Inputs:
- Product category
- Capacity
- MOQ
- Certifications
- Delivery area
- Availability
- Business type
- Purchase frequency

Outputs:
- Compatibility score
- Reasoning
- Suggested partners

AI also summarizes negotiations into structured agreements.

## Groq Backend Integration

Environment variables:

```env
GROQ_API_KEY=
GROQ_MODEL=llama-3.1-8b-instant
GROQ_BASE_URL=https://api.groq.com/openai/v1
ENABLE_AI=true
```

If `GROQ_MODEL` is empty, the backend uses `llama-3.1-8b-instant`. If `ENABLE_AI=false` or `GROQ_API_KEY` is missing, AI endpoints return deterministic fallback responses from database data.

Implemented endpoints:

- `POST /ai/search-history`
- `GET /recommendations`
- `GET /ai/recommendations`
- `POST /ai/matchmaking`
- `GET /agreements/{id}/ai-verification`

The Groq client uses the OpenAI-compatible `/chat/completions` endpoint, request context cancellation, a timeout, low temperature, JSON-only prompting, markdown code fence cleanup, non-2xx handling, empty response handling, and invalid JSON handling. API keys are never logged.

Recommendation rules:

- Search history is treated as interest only.
- Agreement history has stronger weight than searches.
- Confirmed agreements are prioritized.
- Groq may only rank candidate IDs supplied by the backend.
- Private contact information is not sent to Groq.

## Buyer-Producer Matchmaking

Endpoint:

- `POST /ai/matchmaking`

The backend calculates deterministic compatibility before Groq is called. Groq only explains and ranks candidates already scored by the backend.

Weighted score:

- Product category match: 25
- Producer capacity satisfies buyer quantity: 15
- Producer MOQ does not exceed buyer quantity: 10
- Required certifications are available: 15
- Delivery area covers buyer location: 10
- Availability fits buyer timeline: 10
- Business type compatibility: 5
- Purchase frequency compatibility: 5
- Distance proximity: 5, only when coordinate data exists

Hard constraints:

- Product category must be compatible.
- Producer capacity must satisfy buyer quantity.
- Producer MOQ must not exceed buyer quantity.
- Producer must support the buyer delivery area.
- Required certifications must be available.
- Availability must fit the buyer need date.

The current schema stores city/location strings but no coordinates, so distance is returned as unavailable and the backend does not ask Groq to infer geographic distance from city names.

Match status:

- `90-100`: `excellent_match`
- `75-89`: `good_match`
- `50-74`: `partial_match`
- `0-49`: `poor_match`

If a critical hard constraint fails, the backend prevents `excellent_match` even if Groq returns a higher value. AI scores are capped at the backend deterministic score.

Agreement verification summarizes negotiation messages and agreement line items before contact reveal. Contact reveal is considered allowed only when the agreement is `CONFIRMED` and both buyer and producer have confirmed.

## Agreement Verification and Negotiation Summary

Endpoints:

- `GET /agreements/{id}/negotiation-summary`
- `GET /agreements/{id}/ai-verification`
- `POST /agreements/{id}/ai-verification`

`GET /agreements/{id}/negotiation-summary` summarizes messages from the chat room attached to the agreement match. The backend limits the message window and sends only negotiation messages for that agreement's match.

`POST /agreements/{id}/ai-verification` compares buyer and producer submissions. AI only recommends verification. The backend controls `can_reveal_contact`.

Contact/profile reveal requires:

- Authenticated requester.
- Valid agreement ID.
- Requester is buyer or producer in the agreement match.
- Buyer submission exists.
- Producer submission exists.
- Critical fields match after backend normalization.
- Additional terms are compatible.
- Buyer final confirm is true.
- Producer final confirm is true.
- Stored agreement status is `CONFIRMED`.
- Stored buyer and producer confirmation flags are both true.
- AI recommendation does not contradict the backend deterministic comparison.

Critical fields compared:

- Buyer company
- Producer company
- Product
- Quantity and unit
- Price and currency
- Delivery area or schedule
- Payment terms
- Additional terms

The backend normalizes company names, units, currency, quantities, and unit-vs-total price basis before calling Groq. Groq may compare semantic meaning, but it cannot unlock contact/profile data.
